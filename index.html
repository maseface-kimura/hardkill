The Interceptor Crisis
The single most strategically significant and least publicly discussed fact of this conflict: the US and Israel are consuming interceptors faster than they can produce them. Iran's doctrine is specifically designed to exploit this constraint.

Strait of Hormuz — Iran's Nuclear Option
The Strait of Hormuz is Iran's ultimate strategic weapon. It requires no missiles, no aircraft, no carriers. The credible threat of closure reshapes the entire geopolitical calculus. Understanding it is understanding why Iran cannot simply be bombed into submission.

The Cost of War
The economics of this conflict are one of Iran's most powerful weapons. The exchange rate between cheap Iranian systems and expensive American/Israeli responses is unsustainable over any extended conflict period.
The Houthi campaign in the Red Sea alone forced the US Navy to fire hundreds of millions of dollars in SM-2 missiles against drones costing under $10,000 each. USS Carney fired $2.1M missiles to intercept $10K drones. Iran can do this math all day. Washington apparently cannot.
